When was Daya Learners School of Driver Training established?

Daya Learners School of Driver Training was established on the 16th of September, 1966, in Colombo. It was founded by M/s Daya Rupasinghe, Sri Lanka’s first female heavy vehicle and motorcycle license holder.

Who founded Daya Learners and what is her story?

The school was founded by M/s Daya Rupasinghe, who began driving at a young age with her father’s car. In 1964, she made history by becoming the first woman in Sri Lanka to obtain a heavy vehicle license by driving a bus. Her passion for teaching driving and her expertise led her to establish Daya Learners in 1966.

What makes Daya Learners School unique in driver training?

Daya Learners takes a personalized approach to driver education, tailoring lessons to each student’s individual needs. Whether you're a nervous beginner or an experienced driver looking to enhance your skills, our certified and friendly instructors ensure that you receive training suited to your level.

What is the primary focus of Daya Learners School's driver education?

TOur primary focus is on safety, confidence, and discipline in driving. We use modern teaching methods and provide guidance for learners of all ages, from teenagers to adults, ensuring they become responsible and safe drivers.
